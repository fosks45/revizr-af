# Revizr — Copy and Tone Guidelines

## 1. Brand Voice — Core Principles

Revizr's voice is built on four pillars. Every word written for the product must
pass this test: is it honest, practical, kind, and confident?

**Honest** — Revizr says what the product does, accurately. No inflated claims
about AI magical powers. No grade guarantees. No manufactured urgency. If there's
a limitation, state it clearly and helpfully.

**Practical** — Revizr gets to the point. One clear instruction per sentence. No
filler words. The student has 20 minutes to revise; the copy doesn't waste a
second of it.

**Kind** — Mistakes are how learning happens. The product is on the student's side.
Never shame, never guilt, never pressure. Encouragement is earned through genuine
progress, not manufactured — "You improved your Fractions score" not "Amazing
work, superstar!"

**Confident** — The product knows its purpose. It doesn't hedge or apologise for
existing. It doesn't beg users to upgrade. It knows what it offers is genuinely
useful and says so plainly.

## 2. Voice Spectrum — By Audience

Revizr serves users aged 9 to 18, plus parents and teachers. The brand voice
stays the same but the register shifts.

### 2.1 Under-11 (11+ cohort — Amara, age 9–11)

**Register:** Simple, warm, direct. Maximum two syllables per keyword. Short
sentences. Active verbs.

**Reading level target:** UK Year 4–5 comprehension (Flesch-Kincaid Grade 4–5).

**What to do:**
- Use "you" not "the student"
- Use concrete specifics: "You got 8 out of 10" not "You achieved 80%"
- Name the topic plainly: "Number questions" not "Number and Place Value (N-VP)"
- Use "good try" or "nearly there" not "incorrect"
- Use encouraging micro-copy after any answer, correct or not: "Here's how to
  do it" — never "You got that wrong"
- Short paragraphs — max 2 sentences per thought
- Avoid: percentages (use fractions or counts), exam board jargon, "assessment
  objectives," "mark scheme criteria"

**Examples:**

| Instead of | Say |
|-----------|-----|
| "Your diagnostic is complete" | "We've found your practice topics!" |
| "You have answered incorrectly" | "Not quite — here's the answer" |
| "Commence your practice session" | "Start practising" |
| "Upload your diagnostic evidence" | "Show us your school report" |
| "You have exhausted your free tier allocation" | "You've tried all your free questions on this topic" |

**Error messages (under-11):**

- File upload fail: "That file didn't work. Try a different one or take the quiz instead."
- Network error: "Something went wrong. Check your internet and try again."
- Session save fail: "We couldn't save your answer. Try again."

---

### 2.2 Age 11–16 (KS3 and GCSE — Jack, age 15)

**Register:** Direct, peer-level, no condescension. Short. Action-oriented.

**Reading level target:** UK Year 7–9 comprehension.

**What to do:**
- Treat the student as capable — they're studying for real exams
- Use percentages — they understand them
- Name exam boards correctly (AQA, Edexcel — no invented abbreviations)
- Keep instructions brief: one sentence per step
- Encouragement is factual: "Your Fractions score is up 12 points"
- Do not moralize about revision habits

**Examples:**

| Instead of | Say |
|-----------|-----|
| "Session performance metrics" | "Your score" |
| "You have demonstrated a weak understanding of..." | "This topic needs the most work" |
| "Please complete the assessment" | "Answer the questions to build your topic map" |
| "Insufficient data for topic analysis" | "We need a few more answers to map this topic accurately" |

**Error messages (11–16):**

- File upload fail: "We couldn't read this file. Try a different format or take the quiz instead."
- Network error: "Something went wrong. Check your connection and try again."
- No questions available: "You've practised all available questions on this topic. More are on their way."

---

### 2.3 Age 16–18 (A-level — Tariq, age 17)

**Register:** Factual, concise. Near-adult. No hand-holding but also no corporate
coldness.

**Reading level target:** UK Year 11–12.

**What to do:**
- Speak plainly — Tariq is sophisticated and will notice if the copy is dumbed down
- Use subject-specific vocabulary when it appears in questions (from the exam board
  paper) but explain non-obvious Revizr-specific terms on first use
- Progress data can be more detailed: topic trajectory, spec coverage, revision
  distribution
- Encouragement is analytical: "You've improved on 3 out of 5 weak topics this week"

**Examples:**

| Instead of | Say |
|-----------|-----|
| "Wonderful effort today!" | "Good session — 3 topics improved" |
| "This topic needs attention" | "Organic Chemistry: your weakest current topic — 8 questions available" |

**Error messages (A-level):**

Same pattern as GCSE — direct and actionable.

---

### 2.4 Parents (Priya, Siobhan)

**Register:** Respectful adult-to-adult. Clear. No jargon. Think "letter from a
well-regarded school."

**Key principles:**
- Translate exam-board language for parents: "Algebra — forming expressions,
  solving equations" not "Algebra (N6.1a–f AQA GCSE Higher)"
- Progress summaries use directional language, never raw grades: "on track,"
  "needs some attention," "significant improvement"
- Never suggest a child is failing — always frame as "where to focus"
- Be specific about data: "3 sessions this week, 14 questions, 73% average"
  not vague reassurances

**Parent progress summary voice (F10):**

- Always starts from the positive: "Aaryan has been practising regularly…"
- Names topics in plain English: "Fractions" not "Number — Fractions, Decimals
  and Percentages (N2)"
- Uses relative direction: "This is his strongest area," "This is where he needs
  the most practice"
- Avoids prediction: "on track" not "should get a Grade 6" — per spec F10
  acceptance criteria

**Examples:**

| Instead of | Say |
|-----------|-----|
| "Student session completion metrics for period" | "Aaryan's sessions last week" |
| "Assessment of topic weakness vectors" | "Where Aaryan needs the most practice" |
| "Academic trajectory insufficient for target attainment" | "This area needs more attention before the exam" |
| "No session data in reporting period" | "Aaryan hasn't had a session yet" |

---

### 2.5 Teachers and Tutors (Denise)

**Register:** Professional peer-to-peer. Concise. Data-first.

**What to do:**
- Present data as teachers read it: by topic, by date, by score
- Use standard exam terminology (Denise knows what "AO1" means)
- No marketing language in the teacher portal
- Share links and privacy notices use formal, clear legal language

## 3. Encouragement Rules

Encouragement in Revizr must be:

1. **Earned** — only praise genuine progress, not effort for its own sake
   - YES: "Your Algebra score is up from 43% to 58% this week"
   - NO: "Amazing work! You're a revision superstar!"

2. **Specific** — name the topic, the number, the direction
   - YES: "Fractions: 3 questions in a row correct — looking good"
   - NO: "Great job today!"

3. **Non-comparative** — never compare the student to others
   - Never: "You're in the top 25% of GCSE students"
   - Always: relative to their own past performance

4. **Non-compulsive** — encouragement never creates anxiety about not returning
   - NO streak messaging: "You'll lose your streak if you don't practise today"
   - YES neutral streak display: "5 days this week" (factual count, no threat)

5. **Age-appropriate warmth:**
   - Under-11: warm, friendly, direct: "Great try! Here's the answer."
   - GCSE: factual-positive: "Good session — score up 8 points"
   - A-level: analytical: "Organic Chemistry improving — 3 sessions in"
   - Parents: reassuring-informative: "Aaryan is making solid progress this week"

## 4. Error Message Patterns

Error messages must: state what happened, explain why (if knowable), give a next
step. Never blame the user.

### Format

```
[What happened] — [Why, if known] — [What to do next]
```

### Examples by type

**File / upload errors:**
- "We couldn't read this file — it may be password-protected or too small to
  read. Try a different file or take the quiz instead."
- "This file is too large — maximum 10 MB. Try a smaller file."

**Form validation errors:**
- "Enter your email address" — not "Email field is required"
- "Enter a password of at least 10 characters" — not "Password too short"
- "This email is already registered — [Sign in instead?]"

**Network / connectivity errors:**
- "Something went wrong — check your internet connection and try again."
- "Your progress is saved. Reconnect to continue."

**Payment errors:**
- "Your payment didn't go through — check your card details and try again."
- "Something went wrong on our end — please try again in a moment. [Contact support]"

**Session / state errors:**
- "We couldn't load this question — [Try again] or [Skip to next]"
- "This session has timed out. Your progress is saved — [Resume session]"

**Empty states (not errors, but no-data states):**
- Session history empty: "No sessions yet. Start your first session to see your
  history here. [Start revision]"
- Parent dashboard, no sessions: "Aaryan hasn't started yet. Once he does, you'll
  see his progress here."
- Topic map, no diagnostic: "Your topic map isn't ready yet. Take the quick quiz
  to find your focus areas. [Start quiz]"

## 5. Prohibited Language

The following patterns are banned in all Revizr copy:

| Pattern | Reason | Alternative |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| Countdown timers on consent or upgrade screens | AADC Standard 13 / dark pattern | No time pressure copy |
| "You'll lose your streak if..." | AADC Standard 13 — compulsive design | Factual streak display only |
| "Only X days left in your trial" | No time limit on free tier per spec | N/A — free tier is genuinely unlimited |
| "Most students improve with..." | Normative peer comparison — AADC | Student's own progress only |
| "Your child is falling behind" | Shame framing | "This area needs more attention" |
| "Act now" / "Don't miss out" | Urgency manipulation | Plain benefits statement |
| "Wrong" as standalone feedback | Shame framing | "Not quite" / "Here's the answer" |
| Grade predictions ("should get Grade 8") | Not supported by data; creates false expectations | Directional language only: "on track" |
| "This is your last free question" (when questions remain) | Manufactured scarcity | Show count factually when 1 remains |
| Any copy that implies cancellation is difficult | Dark pattern | "Cancel any time" stated prominently |

## 6. Microcopy Reference

### Button labels

| Action | Label |
|--------|-------|
| Start practice session | "Start revision" (not "Begin assessment") |
| Continue a paused session | "Resume session" |
| Submit an answer | "Submit answer" |
| Advance to next question | "Next question" |
| End the session | "End session" |
| View mark scheme | (shown automatically — no button needed) |
| Upgrade subscription | "See plans and pricing" (not "Upgrade now") |
| Cancel subscription | "Cancel subscription" (not buried, not "Downgrade") |
| Keep subscription | "Keep my plan" (honest) |
| Sign in | "Sign in" (not "Log in") |
| Sign out | "Sign out" |
| Delete account | "Delete my account" (not "Remove account") |

### Navigation labels

| Destination | Label |
|-------------|-------|
| Student home | "Home" |
| Weakness map | "My Topics" |
| Session history | "History" |
| Progress charts | "Progress" |
| Account settings | "Account" |
| Parent dashboard | "Dashboard" |
| Individual child view | Child's first name |

### Welsh (cy) locale

All UI copy must be translated by a native Welsh speaker or qualified translator
before launch. Do not auto-translate with machine translation alone. Key terms
require linguist review:

- "Topic map" → "Map pynciau" (verify with translator)
- "Session" → "Sesiwn" (verify)
- "Mark scheme" → "Cynllun marcio" (verify)
- "Exam board" → "Bwrdd arholi" (verify)

Exam board names (AQA, WJEC, CCEA, OCR, Edexcel) are proper nouns — do not
translate; use in original form with Welsh context where needed.

## 7. Tone by Screen Type

| Screen Type | Tone | Priority |
|------------|------|----------|
| Onboarding | Welcoming, clear, brief | Reduce anxiety, set expectations |
| Diagnostic / quiz | Focused, neutral | Keep attention on the task |
| Processing (loading) | Calm, informative | Manage waiting without frustration |
| Question screen | Neutral — content leads | The question is the hero |
| Mark scheme reveal | Instructive, supportive | Learning moment |
| Session complete | Warm, factual | Acknowledge effort, show progress |
| Weakness map | Informational, action-oriented | "Here's what to do next" |
| Parent dashboard | Informative, reassuring | Plain English summary |
| Upgrade / subscription | Confident, transparent | No pressure, clear value |
| Error | Helpful, calm, actionable | Next step always clear |
| Settings / account | Functional, clear | Efficiency — user knows what they want |
