# SWOT Synthesis Gate Decision — Revizr (002-revizr)

## Verdict: PASSED

The SWOT synthesis confirms that Revizr's internal strengths and external opportunity set constitute a commercially viable and defensible basis for proceeding through the product pipeline. No finding in either the SWOT quadrants or the PESTEL analysis meets the criteria for a fundamental threat that would require escalation to the human Portfolio owner or pipeline termination.

The analysis identifies three genuine hard blockers that are already captured as open items in the market-pack decision: exam board copyright licensing (L1/W2), child data compliance under the ICO Children's Code (L2/W4), and AI report-parsing accuracy validation (W1). All three are known, assigned to downstream squads (Compliance for L1/L2; Architecture for W1), and are not new findings — they are confirmed priorities, not kill conditions.

The five SO strategies (authentic-paper brand leadership, 11+ entry segment, cost-of-living framing, devolved nations positioning, D2C-to-B2B flywheel) are coherent, mutually reinforcing, and executable within Revizr's current asset base. The five WT mitigations (copyright resolved before launch, accuracy spike before build, engagement mechanics from Sprint 1, Children's Code sign-off before first user, brand-building timed against Seneca's replication window) translate directly into sequenced workstreams for the Architecture, Compliance, and UX/Design squads.

No fundamental external threat — exam board self-platforming, Seneca acquisition of a paper database, AI commoditisation of the diagnosis layer — is imminent within the Year 1 operating window. These risks are correctly classified as medium-term watch items, not blocking conditions.

Human approval is not required at this gate under the constitution (§2). The gate does not touch auth, payments, production personal data systems, or constitution amendments. The C7 child data compliance gate (which does require human approval) is a downstream milestone within the Compliance squad workstream and remains open and correctly tracked.

**Advance to: Architecture (Squad 7) and Compliance (Squad 4, copyright and children's data tracks) in parallel, with UX/Design (Squad 6) beginning the engagement and conversion flow design workstream.**
